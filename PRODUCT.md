# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Scout serves job seekers who are busy, exhausted by repetitive applications, or have already tried applying alone without getting enough traction. They do not need another job-search productivity tool; they need the application work handled for them while they retain visibility and control.

## Product Purpose

Scout is a managed job-application service with two fulfillment lanes:

1. Human Assistant — a person applies on the member's behalf, communicates through a dedicated WhatsApp group, and provides detailed evidence of completed application forms.
2. AI Assistant — a lower-cost agent applies on the member's behalf and records the job, status, and resume used.

Both lanes use the same profile system, dashboard, job delegation workflow, tailored documents, and browser extension.

## Conversion Paths

### Human Assistant

The primary call to action is **Book a free strategy call**.

1. The member books a call.
2. Scout learns their target roles, locations, preferences, dealbreakers, and resume strategy.
3. Scout assigns a human assistant and creates a dedicated WhatsApp group.
4. The assistant begins applying.
5. The member sees every job in the dashboard, the resume used, and detailed evidence or screenshots of completed form answers.
6. The member can add specific jobs and choose the job profile or resume the assistant should use.

### AI Assistant

The primary call to action is **Start with AI**.

1. The member creates an account with Google or email.
2. They choose AI Assistant.
3. They upload a resume.
4. Scout analyzes it and visibly extracts experience, education, skills, and repeated application answers.
5. Scout creates the first job profile and opens the dashboard.
6. The AI Assistant applies and records the job, status, and resume used. Detailed screenshots of form answers are not included in this lane.

### Direct Registration

Members who register without booking a call sign in with Google or email, select Human or AI, upload a resume, review the extracted data, and enter the prepared dashboard. Human members can still schedule their kickoff call from onboarding.

## Core Product Objects

- Job profiles: multiple role/location/search strategies per member.
- Resumes: original resumes plus tailored versions linked to jobs and profiles.
- Applications: job, company, assistant lane, status, submission time, and resume used.
- Human evidence: screenshots or records of form answers.
- Delegated jobs: jobs added from the dashboard or browser extension.
- Assistant conversation: dedicated WhatsApp group for human-assistant members.
- Browser extension: add any job found on the web and select the job profile to use.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, operational, relieving, and human. Scout should feel like a competent application desk running in the background, not a novelty chatbot or a generic productivity app.

## Messaging Hierarchy

1. Outcome: we apply to jobs for you.
2. Empathy: you are busy or exhausted; the repetition is the problem.
3. Choice: use a human assistant or a lower-cost AI assistant.
4. Visibility: see the job, materials, status, and lane-specific proof.
5. Control: multiple profiles, preferred resume behavior, add any job.
6. Conversion: book a call for Human; start immediately for AI.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with relief from application work, not AI technology.
2. Never merge the two primary calls to action into a vague “get started.”
3. Explain the service through the actual member journey.
4. Show operational artifacts: job profiles, resume choices, form evidence, WhatsApp, queues, and receipts.
5. Make the evidence difference between Human and AI explicit.
6. Preserve a clear route back to the next action after every major section.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, clear focus states, reduced-motion support, and plain-language copy. Motion should clarify workflow and status only; the experience must remain complete when animations are reduced.
